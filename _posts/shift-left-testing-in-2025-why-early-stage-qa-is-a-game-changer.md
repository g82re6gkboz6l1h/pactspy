## What is Shift-Left Testing?
### The Shift-Left Principle
Shift-Left Testing revolves around moving QA processes earlier—or "leftward"—in the timeline of software development. Unlike traditional methodologies that address bugs late in the SDLC, this approach integrates testing during initial planning and design phases, preventing issues before they escalate.

#### Key Practices:
- **Behavior-Driven Development (BDD):** Collaborative requirement definition involving QA teams.
- **Continuous Testing:** Automating tests throughout development.
- **Team Collaboration:** Encouraging communication between developers, testers, and product stakeholders.

Benefits include reduced risks, improved software reliability, and faster product delivery.
