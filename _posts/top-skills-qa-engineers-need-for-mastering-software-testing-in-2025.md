Core Skills Needed for Automation & AI-Focused QA Roles

As automation and AI reshape software testing processes, QA engineers must develop technical expertise to meet industry demands.

### Automation Mastery:
- Proficiency with testing frameworks: Selenium, Appium, TestNG.
- Skilled in script writing using Python, JavaScript, or Java.
- Knowledge of cloud-based automation for scalability.

### AI-Centric Skills:
- AI model training and data feeding techniques.
- Recognizing patterns for predictive and behavioral testing.
- Integrating AI tools into DevOps pipelines.

The combination of automation knowledge and AI literacy will set QA engineers apart in 2025.

Upskilling for Predictive and Behavior-Driven Testing

Predictive testing and behavior-driven methodologies are emerging as pivotal approaches in software testing.

### Upskilling Areas:
1. **Predictive Testing**:
   - Learn data analytics tools such as Power BI and Tableau.
   - Build risk assessment expertise to prioritize critical areas.

2. **Behavior-Driven Testing**:
   - Collaborate closely with UX teams for user-centric designs.
   - Master BDD tools like Cucumber or Behave.

Investing time in predictive and behavioral testing will prepare QA engineers for real-world application challenges.
