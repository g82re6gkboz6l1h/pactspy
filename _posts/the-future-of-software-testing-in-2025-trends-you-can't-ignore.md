Shift-Left Testing: Why It’s the Future of QA

The shift-left testing approach is gaining traction, particularly within Agile and DevOps frameworks. This approach involves initiating testing early in the development cycle, during the design and coding phases, to prevent downstream issues and reduce risks.

### Advantages of Shift-Left Testing:

- **Early Bug Identification**: Fixing defects during coding is faster and more cost-effective than addressing them post-deployment.
- **Accelerated Feedback:** Real-time feedback fosters iterative improvements throughout the development process.
- **Higher Efficiency**: Ensures testing and development teams collaborate effectively, reducing miscommunication.
